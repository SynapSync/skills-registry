# Focus Modes

Optional modifiers that adjust the analysis lens. When a user activates a focus mode, apply that perspective as the **primary lens** across all analysis steps while still covering the full methodology.

A focus mode does not skip steps — it changes the emphasis and depth within each step.

---

## CTO Mode

**Lens:** Technical depth and architecture decisions.

- Emphasize system design, scalability constraints, and technical trade-offs
- Go deeper on Step 6 (System Design) and ADR creation
- Evaluate build-vs-buy decisions with cost/complexity analysis
- Assess team technical capabilities and skill gaps
- Prioritize architectural sustainability over feature velocity

---

## Product Mode

**Lens:** UX, user flows, and product priorities.

- Emphasize Step 7 (UX/Product) and Step 5 (MVP Proposal)
- Map user journeys and identify friction points
- Prioritize features by user impact, not technical elegance
- Focus on retention metrics and user activation
- Challenge feature requests against actual user problems

---

## Growth Mode

**Lens:** Acquisition, retention, and growth loops.

- Emphasize Step 8 (Viability/Market) and Step 3 (Strategic Value)
- Identify viral loops, network effects, and organic growth channels
- Analyze unit economics and CAC/LTV dynamics
- Map the funnel: awareness > acquisition > activation > retention > revenue > referral
- Prioritize features that compound growth

---

## Investor Mode

**Lens:** Metrics, narrative, and risk assessment.

- Emphasize Step 8 (Viability/Market) and Step 4 (Evolutionary Vision)
- Frame analysis as an investment thesis
- Quantify TAM/SAM/SOM where applicable
- Assess competitive moat and defensibility
- Identify key risks and mitigation strategies
- Build a narrative arc: problem > insight > solution > traction > vision

---

## Technical Mode

**Lens:** Systems, scalability, and implementation details.

- Emphasize Step 6 (System Design) and Step 2 (Critical Diagnosis)
- Deep-dive into architecture patterns and trade-offs
- Evaluate current tech stack fitness for strategic goals
- Assess operational complexity and DevOps readiness
- Focus on performance, reliability, and security posture

---

## Brutal Mode

**Lens:** Honest critique without sugarcoating.

- Apply maximum skepticism to every assumption
- Challenge the user's framing of the problem
- Point out uncomfortable truths about market fit, execution risk, and technical debt
- No "it depends" — commit to a position and defend it
- Rank risks by severity, not by how comfortable they are to discuss
- Still constructive: every critique must include a path forward
